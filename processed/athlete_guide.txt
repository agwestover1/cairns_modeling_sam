WELCOME MESSAGE
Welcome to IRONMAN Cairns 2026

To every athlete, supporter, volunteer, official and visitor joining us for this iconic event, welcome
to Tropical North Queensland and welcome to one of the most spectacular race destinations in
the world.

There is something special about IRONMAN Cairns.

Maybe it’s the sunrise over the Coral Sea.

The energy that builds across the Esplanade before dawn.

The cheers from complete strangers carrying athletes through every tough moment.

Or perhaps it’s the unique combination of challenge and beauty that only this region can offer.

From the world-famous swim course to the breathtaking ride along the Captain Cook Highway,
one of the most picturesque coastal drives in the country, and the electric atmosphere along the
Cairns waterfront, this event showcases our region at its very best.

Whether you are competing for a personal milestone, a world championship qualification spot or
simply to prove something to yourself, every person standing on that start line has their own story,
their own reason and their own journey.

That is what makes IRONMAN so powerful.

Cairns is proud to host this event for the fifteenth year and proud to welcome competitors from
across Australia and around the globe to our community.

While you are here, I encourage you to experience everything our region has to offer.

Explore our restaurants and local businesses, connect with our people and immerse yourself in
the natural beauty that surrounds us, from the Great Barrier Reef to the world’s oldest living
rainforest.

Most importantly, soak it all in.

Good luck, race hard and enjoy every moment.




Amy Eden
Mayor of Cairns

IMPORTANT MEDICAL AND HEALTH INFORMATION

•      MEDICAL INFORMATION

      •      If you have a known medical condition, we highly advise that you take the following steps:

             1.     Please stop by the Help Desk at Athlete Check-In to get a special race day wristband on
                    which you should write the details of your medical conditions(s).

             2.     Please also write the details of your medical conditions(s) on the back of your athlete bib,
                    which you will receive in your race packet.

             The medical information that is placed on your wristband and athlete bib will provide medical staff
             or emergency services with essential information. If you have any questions or concerns about
             providing medical information, please stop at the Help Desk at Athlete Check-In.

•   Your safety is our primary concern. The medical team will be staffing the medical facilities at Cairns
    Esplanade. Medical resources will also be available while you are out on course.
•   If you are not feeling well on race morning, we strongly advise you not to start the event. If you decide to
    not start the event, you must inform a race official so we can officially withdraw you from the event.
•   If you start to feel unwell at any point during race day, we urge you to seek help rather than continue.


                                       DO NOT BE AFRAID TO ASK FOR HELP
                  You will not receive a penalty or be disqualified for receiving a medical evaluation.


If at any time during the race you experience chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, lightheadedness
(dizziness), numbness around the mouth or in hands, difficulty concentrating, or stop sweating please stop
immediately and seek medical attention. A medical evaluation does not constitute outside assistance. You will
not receive a penalty or be disqualified for receiving a medical evaluation, or for having someone help you in
getting medical assistance.

       ○    During the swim: raise and wave your hand, which will alert the water safety team that you need
            help. If able to, you can also verbalize you are in need of help by yelling “help.” You can also grab
            onto a kayak, paddle board or nearby boat. You will not be disqualified for grabbing onto any of
            these safety items during the race.
       ○    During the bike: If you are at an aid station, let someone know you are in need of medical help. If
            you are not at or near an aid station – STOP – let someone know you need medical help. Anyone
            can help - another athlete, a volunteer, a staff vehicle, event support vehicle or event moto out on
            course; even a spectator. Have them alert the nearest aid station or staff member that you need
            medical assistance. Make sure you provide your bib number to initiate your care.
       ○    During the run: same as the bike. If you are at an aid station, let someone know you are in need of
            medical help. If you are not at or near an aid station – STOP – let someone know you need medical
            help. Anyone can help - another athlete, a volunteer, a staff vehicle, event support vehicle or event
            moto out on course; even a spectator. Have them alert the nearest aid station or staff member that
            you need medical assistance. Make sure you provide your bib number to initiate your care.
       ○    Helping another athlete – if a fellow athlete needs help, please use the same steps listed above to
            get the athlete medical assistance. You will not be disqualified or receive a penalty for assisting a
            fellow athlete in medical need. On the bike and run course, make sure to provide the bib number
            of the athlete in need of help.

     After you have finished the race, please do not leave the post-race area until you are able to drink without
      vomiting, stand without dizziness and feel okay. If you are not feeling well, please come to the event
      medical tent to be evaluated by the medical team before leaving the recovery area.

COURSE
The 3.8km ROKA Swim course is situated at the
stunning location of Palm Cove. The course is 2
laps with Swim Start off Williams Esplanade and
Swim Exit further North towards transition. This
year, the course has been pulled closer to the
shore to lessen the effects of any potential
winds and make for a smoother swim. We have
also added more buoys to help with you
sighting while out on the course. IRONMAN
athletes will start their swim by heading out and
turning right at the first white turning buoy,
from here on out, all other buoys will be on your
left-hand side. Pink-coloured buoys will mark
where you need to turn, and red and orange
buoys will be used for sighting and to keep you
on course. Athletes will start their second lap
when turning at the 3rd pink buoy. On their
second approach to the 3rd pink buoy, athletes
will continue to swim to towards the white exit
buoy before leaving the water towards T1.

OFFICIAL EVENT APP
The IRONMAN Athlete Tracker app is your go-to source for live athlete tracking,
leaderboards, and event information. Simply download, select the event you wish to
follow, and identify yourself as a fan/spectator or race participant. Then, hit the Leaders
button to check in on the progress of the entire pro field.
Alternatively, hit the Tracker button and type in your favourite athlete’s name to receive
progress updates, splits, and finish times. Download for free on iOS or Android, select
your race, search for your athlete, and receive notifications as they happen.
• Live Web Tracker
See times and current pace within seconds of a participant crossing each split point.
Estimated times will be provided based on current pace.
• Live Leaderboard
See who is winning via the Live Leaderboard. Top athletes in each category will be
displayed in real-time as they lead the way through the course.
• Live Map Tracking
Athlete locations will be plotted on the Interactive Course Map as progress is made on
course. The position of each participant is estimated based on the most recent timing
received from the timing chip read.
•   Real-time Notifications
Get automatic posting as progress is made on the course. As a spectator, the times of
the participant you are tracking can be pushed to your phone or posted to your X
(Twitter) feed automatically allowing friends and family to share your excitement!
NOTE: Times and results posted during the race are unofficial. Official results are posted
to the website upon completion and official verification of the event.
